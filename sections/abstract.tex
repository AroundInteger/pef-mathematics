\begin{abstract}
The companion empirical paper generalises Fisher's paired efficiency to unequal variances through the Paired Efficiency Factor $\eta = (1+\kappa)/(1+\kappa-2\sqrt{\kappa}\,\rho)$, where $\kappa$ is the variance ratio and $\rho$ the intra-pair correlation.  This paper studies the mathematical structure of the formula.

The substitution $\tau = \tfrac{1}{2}\log\kappa$ yields the canonical form $\eta = \cosh\tau/(\cosh\tau - \rho)$, from which four structures follow as corollaries: (\textit{i}) the map $\kappa \mapsto 1/\kappa$ acts trivially on $\eta$, so the empirical four-quadrant taxonomy is a two-to-one covering of a fundamental half-strip; (\textit{ii}) the reciprocal $1/\eta = 1 - \rho\,\sech\tau$ is affine in $\rho$ and admits a Poisson-kernel representation on the disc together with a geometric/Chebyshev series expansion; (\textit{iii}) setting $\cos\sigma = \rho\,\sech\tau$ realises each $(\kappa,\rho)$ pair as a point on the Riemann sphere at angular distance $\sigma$ from a marked pole, with $\eta = 1/(1-\cos\sigma)$; and (\textit{iv}) the identity $\log\eta = -\log(1-\rho\,\sech\tau)$ is the log-partition function of a geometric exponential family with natural parameter $\theta = \log(\rho\,\sech\tau)$, giving distribution-free cumulant identities $\mathbb{E}[N] = \eta - 1$ and $\Var[N] = \eta(\eta-1)$.  From the Fisher--Rao metric on this family, the coordinate $\psi = 2\,\arctanh\!\sqrt{\rho\,\sech\tau}$ is variance-stabilising, with $\Var(\hat\psi) \approx 1/n$ uniformly across the half-strip, and is the natural scale for cross-domain meta-analysis.

All five structures are validated numerically on the six datasets of the empirical companion (113 sports KPI studies and 19\,903 non-sports units): the $\kappa \leftrightarrow 1/\kappa$ symmetry holds at machine precision (maximum residual $1.78\times10^{-15}$); the sphere equivalence holds at machine precision (maximum residual $8.88\times10^{-16}$); bootstrap confidence-interval widths scale as $1/\sqrt{n}$ across domains; and the predicted regime change at $\rho = 0$ is detected in machine-learning residuals.  These results are distribution-free and hold for any variance ratio and correlation for which the denominator of $\eta$ is non-zero.
\end{abstract}
